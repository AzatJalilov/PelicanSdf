# Code of Conduct

Pelican SDF is an open technical and creative project. We want participation to
feel safe, rigorous, and welcoming regardless of experience, identity, background,
or point of view.

## Expected behavior

- Be kind, specific, and constructive.
- Critique artifacts, methodology, and code—not the people who made them.
- Treat benchmark limitations and negative results as useful evidence.
- Disclose conflicts of interest and model/provider affiliations when relevant.
- Respect privacy, authorship, licensing, and requests to correct attribution.
- Make space for newcomers and explain project-specific terminology.

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized attention, doxxing, sustained
disruption, and deliberately misleading benchmark claims are not acceptable.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove contributions, comments, issues, or other behavior
that violates this code. Responses may range from a private clarification to a
temporary or permanent ban, depending on severity and history. Good-faith reports
will be handled as privately and promptly as practical, with care for the safety
of the reporter.

Until the repository has named conduct contacts, report an incident privately to
the repository owner through the hosting platform. Do not post sensitive reports
in a public issue. Maintainers must recuse themselves when they are involved in a
report.

This code applies in project spaces and when someone is publicly representing the
project.
